Teaching Philosophy

Siphokazi Janet Ralane
Accredited Assessor (Services SETA) • Code 8 Driver’s License

Languages: IsiXhosa (Excellent), English (Excellent), IsiZulu (Good), Afrikaans (Fair)

My philosophy is grounded in student-centered learning, constructive alignment, and authentic assessment. I aim to cultivate independent, reflective graduates who can apply management concepts in real contexts.

  • Design learning experiences that link theory to practice (case studies from South African enterprises).
  • Create inclusive classrooms where language diversity and varied backgrounds are valued.
  • Emphasize feedback loops—feedforward guidance that improves subsequent performance.
“Tell me and I forget. Teach me and I remember. Involve me and I learn.”

Design for Learning Strategies

I use backward design to align intended learning outcomes (ILOs), assessments, and activities.

OutcomeAssessmentLearning Activities
Apply strategic & quantitative management tools Applied case analysis Workshops, simulations, problem sets
Demonstrate entrepreneurial thinking Venture pitch & WIL reflections Design sprints, peer review, industry mentor talks

Facilitating Learning

To facilitate learning, I blend short lectures with active methods:

  • Case-based learning: Local SA cases to contextualize theory.
  • WIL integration: Structured pre-briefs, on-site tasks, and reflective debriefs.
  • Peer instruction: Think–pair–share and poll-based concept checks.
  • Accessibility: Notes & slides uploaded in advance; scaffolding for multilingual learners.

Assessment & Feedback

Assessment is transparent and criterion-referenced. Feedback is timely, constructive, and actionable (CAP: Constructive, Actionable, Prompt).

  • Clear rubrics, exemplars, and marking guides shared before tasks.
  • Audio/text feedback with feedforward points for the next submission.
  • Moderation and reflection notes to continuously improve assessment design.

Evaluating Teaching & Learning

I evaluate teaching through multiple lenses:

  • Student feedback (surveys, focus groups) and performance trends.
  • Peer observation and reflective teaching journals.
  • Assessment data (item analysis) to identify misconceptions.
